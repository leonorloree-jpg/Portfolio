Autonomous Multitasking Robot

Completed: Aug 2024 – Nov 2025
Key Skills: Prototyping, Manufacturing, CAD Design, Project Planning, Leadership, CNC

Project Overview

This robot was designed for a university course ME 2110: Creative Decisions and Design. The goal of the robot was to accomplish 3 out of 4 potential tasks collecting and depositing elements under a cost of $120. I managed a group SolidWorks assembly and depositing subsystem at the front of the robot. Over 50% of the robot was manufactured using FDM 3D printing to minimize human fabrication time and provide rapid prototyping.

Rapid Prototyping

A large portion of this project was spent designing and prototyping different mechanisms to accomplish the various tasks. I was responsible for a mechanism to deposit balls at regular intervals and I prototyped my way through a motor-driven design, a solenoid design, and eventually, a fully mechanical design utilizing rubber bands and a launching lever.

We were able to practice so many designs due to the efficiency of FDM printing, manufacturing parts while we took classes or studied.

My favorite part of the project was the design to the left here, a cross section of a machined aluminum core integrated with a 3D printed plastic spool, this design increased our part durability by over 100% for only a 50% increase in part cost.

We got the benefits of cheap plastic for our larger diameter, and more expensive aluminum for the durable core.
