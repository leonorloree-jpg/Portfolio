Transforming Buzz Mascot

Completed: Jan 2025 – May 2025
Key Skills: CAD Design, Leadership, DFMA, Additive Manufacturing

Project Overview and Research

This project was proposed by my school's Advanced Manufacturing Research Lab as a chance to show off their manufacturing capabilities. They planned to design a transformer to stand ~3 feet tall using all of their manufacturing methods from CNC to wire EDM and Powder Bed Fusion.

I started this project on a team of three where we researched the two states, the Ford Model A and our mascot Buzz, to see what mechanisms we could use to bring the two designs together.

Design Process

For the design, I started by finding some reference dimensions on the Ford Model A, and I made a reference CAD model that we could build the Buzz model off of. From there we divided into three subsections, the head, the torso, and the legs.

From there, I started by designing the legs with a fold out design, taking advantage of the tall middle structure to support the model from tipping with a wide base.

While designing the feet, I also took care to ensure the dimensions of each component would fit within the size of our printers, using fasteners to assemble closed components.

Favorite Component

One of my favorite features that I designed was this collapsible tail structure. Using a series of spline curves in SolidWorks, I created these individual curved shells with guide rails. A bumper on the bottom prevents them from coming apart, and the curved profile closely resembles Buzz's stinger!
