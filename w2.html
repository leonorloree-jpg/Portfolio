Mechanical Engineering Intern at Plantd Materials

Duration: May 2025 – August 2025
Key Skills: Manufacturing, CAD Design, Engineering Presentation, Material Testing

What is Plantd Materials?

Between May and August of 2025 I worked at a startup based out of North Carolina called Plantd Materials. Plantd Materials is a carbon-negative company that makes a plywood / OSB product from a perennial grass native to Texas but that can grow in North Carolina. When I joined the company was still ramping up and refining production so I got a chance to get some real hands-on engineering experience starting in the quality engineering department, moving to manufacturing engineering and process design, and ending with some materials testing and part design with experienced design engineers.

One of my favorite parts of this experience was the engineering presentations that I gave. All engineering design decisions, no matter how small, had to be justified with easy-to-understand calculations and CAD exploring all possible avenues of design. I had a lot of ground to cover here in engineering presentation and developing confidence in my decisions that I hadn't needed to have before this internship, so I consider the experience essential.

Design Projects

Some of the projects that I worked on at this internship were conveyor belts for a new raw material processing assembly the company was putting together. Since I was working on these late into the summer, my first priority in design was making the conveyor belt models as modular as possible to ensure easy editing later. This angled conveyor was actually a lot of fun to work with since I learned parametric sketching processes to alter the top overhang angle and update the entire model with a click of a button.

I also got to work on some fixtures for motor assemblies and our primary press (not shown), a stress testing of new belt materials, as well as a chain-mounted pushing assembly with a modular depth that people could set that was used for board post-processing.
